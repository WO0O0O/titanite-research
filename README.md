# Titanite Research: Mapping the Industrial and Geopolitical Inflections of the AGI Transition

## The Thesis: The Physical Reality of the Decade of the OOMs

The scaling laws of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) are no longer confined to the digital realm. As model training and deployment scale by orders of magnitude (OOMs), the primary constraints have shifted from algorithmic design to the physical world: power generation, transmission infrastructure, advanced metrology, and semiconductor supply-chain security.

Titanite Research is a systematic effort to identify, analyse, and track the critical nodes, materials, and equipment manufacturers that underpin this transition. Inspired by the macro-industrial and national security outlook of Leopold Aschenbrenner's _Situational Awareness_ thesis, this repository focuses on the companies that will capture the economics of this capital spend before they are priced in by the consensus.

Our research is organised around three interlocking structural pillars:

1. **Compute & Semiconductor Scaling**: Hardware chokepoints in the advanced packaging, optical interconnect, and substrate layers (e.g. Glass Substrates, high-frequency Metrology, and micro-materials).
2. **Energy & Power Infrastructure**: The physical bottlenecks of the gigawatt-scale data centre. This encompasses power transmission, high-voltage transformers, grid connection hardware, and next-generation liquid cooling systems.
3. **Geopolitical Realignment & Sovereignty**: The decoupling of Western supply chains from Chinese manufacturing, the impact of export controls, and the national security mandates driving domestic capacity expansion.

## Methodology: The Human-Machine Loop

We run a dual-phase research methodology, using automated model sweeps to process vast financial datasets and filing databases, followed by rigorous human-led research to verify the findings and build a complete investment thesis.

### Phase 1: Modular Agentic Sweeps

We build and deploy specialised, prompt-engineered AI agents to run initial company and sector assessments. Currently, the repository utilises:

- **Framework Module 1: Small-Cap AI Infrastructure Chokepoints** (defined in `RESEARCH-NOTES/AGENT.md`). This module targets micro-to-small cap companies (<$5B market cap) in the hardware supply chain, auditing earnings transcripts and regulatory filings for capacity constraints, lead time extensions, and backlog changes.

Future framework modules will expand our agentic capabilities into:

- **Framework Module 2: Energy & Grid Infrastructure Inflections** (identifying grid-level suppliers and power gen bottlenecks).
- **Framework Module 3: Geopolitical Re-shoring & Friend-shoring Rotations** (tracking policy-driven capital flows and supply chain migration).
- **Framework Module 4: Macro Horizon & Trend Forecasting** (modelling longer-term architectural shifts).

### Phase 2: Human-in-the-Loop Reconciliation

The agentic output is a filter, not the final investment decision. We manually stress-test and rebuild the analysis where automated tools fall short:

- **Forensic Accounting**: Verifying auditor credibility, internal controls, related-party transactions, and capital allocation discipline (e.g. share buybacks vs. dilutive raises).
- **Competitive Roadmap Verification**: Scrutinising emerging technology threats, Chinese competitor progress, and customer qualification cycle times.
- **Upside Arithmetic**: Auditing valuation models and peer-group multiple comparisons to ensure candidate companies present genuine asymmetric risk-reward profiles.

## Repository Structure

The research is categorised by thematic bottlenecks:

- **`3D-metrology/`**: Inspection systems and acoustic microscopy required for high-bandwidth memory (HBM) and advanced packaging yield assurance.
- **`MLCC/`**: High-capacitance ceramic capacitors and upstream dielectric materials necessary for GPU power delivery.
- **`TGV/`**: Glass substrates, laser-induced deep etching (LIDE), and advanced packaging equipment for next-generation silicon-to-glass transitions.
- **`photonics/`**: Optical interconnects, external light sources, and silicon photonics materials bypassing electrical speed limits.
- **`advanced-packaging-equip/`**: Substrate processing, vacuum systems, and automated handling tools for 2.5D/3D chip stacking.
