## Key Risks to Thesis

1. **Consumer MLCC hoarding is a cyclical distortion:** The 20–40% spot price increases are partly driven by hoarding/double-booking in China. When this unwinds, it could create an inventory correction that hits all players indiscriminately — even the equipment and materials suppliers

2. **OEM contract prices lag:** The X thread notes OEM contracts haven't seen large price hikes yet. Until this flows through, the profitability benefit to raw material suppliers may be muted

3. **Rapid capacity expansion = oversupply risk in 2–3 years:** The X thread acknowledges "rapid capacity expansion happening across the industry." MLCC cycles historically end badly for suppliers when capacity comes online. Equipment and materials companies get their orders pulled forward — then nothing

4. **Geopolitical / China-A exposure:** Three of the most relevant pure-play companies (Sinocera excluded on cap grounds) are listed in Japan, Taiwan, and Korea. Each carries FX and geopolitical risk. China domestic hoarding may benefit Chinese suppliers in ways that are hard to track from outside

5. **Verification bottleneck:** All these companies require earnings call transcript sweeps (Step A of the prompt.md framework) and proper financial analysis before scoring. The data above is research-phase only — it does not constitute a full Serenity Chokepoint analysis
