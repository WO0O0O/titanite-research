## INTEGRITY FLAG — READ BEFORE PROCEEDING

**MATERIAL WEAKNESS — MONITOR FLAG (Thesis continues; full position not justified until remediation confirmed)**

Nebius Group's 2025 Annual Report (Form 20-F, filed 30 April 2026) contains an **adverse opinion** from its auditor, Reanda Audit & Assurance B.V., regarding the effectiveness of internal control over financial reporting as of 31 December 2025. Two material weaknesses are disclosed:

1. **Property and equipment accounting** — controls over the recognition and valuation of fixed assets (primarily GPU infrastructure) were not effective.
2. **TripleTen segment revenue recognition** — controls over the revenue process for the TripleTen education segment were not effective.

The TripleTen weakness is isolated to a non-core segment that represents a small fraction of group revenue. The property and equipment weakness is more structurally significant: GPU assets are the company's primary operating asset. Nebius has announced it is replacing Reanda with **Deloitte & Touche LLP** beginning with the 2026 fiscal year, and has made no management statement suggesting the property/equipment weakness affects revenue recognition in the core AI cloud business. Nonetheless, this finding cannot be dismissed. It is flagged prominently here per the framework's monitor-flag tier. The analysis proceeds; a full position is not justified until the first Deloitte-audited period confirms the weakness has been remediated.

**Additional historical context:** Arkady Volozh (CEO) was subject to EU sanctions from June 2022 following Russia's invasion of Ukraine. Those sanctions were lifted in **March 2024**, prior to the company relisting as Nebius Group on NASDAQ in October 2024. Volozh renounced his Russian citizenship in **February 2026**. No active sanctions, no SEC investigation, no going-concern opinion. The sanctions history is a known public fact and is not an active disqualifier.

## SECTION 0 — THE OOM TEST

**1. Revenue at 2024 cluster scale (~100k GPUs / 100MW)**

At end-2024, Nebius was generating minimal revenue — the company had only relisted on NASDAQ in October 2024 following the Yandex restructuring. Full-year 2024 revenue was approximately $93M (derived: FY2025 revenue was $530M at 479% growth, implying 2024 base of ~$92M). AI infrastructure revenue at this stage was near-entirely from the legacy European GPU cloud.

**2. Revenue at 1M GPUs / 1GW (2026 cluster scale)**

At 1 GW connected (the company's end-2026 target), the cluster-math implies $8B–$9B in ARR. This is why management is guiding for $7B–$9B ARR at year-end 2026. Nebius's revenue scales **super-linearly** with cluster size in the near term because: (a) its large contractual capacity is partially pre-sold to Meta and Microsoft at fixed rates; (b) as more capacity comes online, deferred revenue converts to recognised revenue at accelerating pace; (c) spot market pricing for GPU capacity remains supply-constrained, allowing yield improvement as volume grows.

**3. Revenue at 10M GPUs / 10GW (2028 cluster scale)**

Nebius has contracted 3.5+ GW as at Q1 2026, targeting 4+ GW by year-end. Its Missouri campus alone is designed for 1.2 GW. The Finland Lappeenranta facility adds 310 MW from 2027. If the company executes 4–5 GW by end-2027 and continues building toward 10 GW, the implied 2028/2029 ARR at that scale is $28B–$50B. Nebius is still in the supply chain at this scale: the Meta $27B and Microsoft $19.4B contracts already carry it well into this range.

**4. Revenue at 100M GPUs / 100GW (2030 cluster scale)**

The key risk at this scale: **hyperscaler vertical integration**. If Microsoft and Meta build their own GPU cloud capacity, they reduce reliance on Nebius. The 5-year contract structures (running to 2030/2031) provide a contractual floor through this transition. The deeper question is whether Nebius secures a next generation of hyperscaler or sovereign contracts beyond those now in place.

**5. Market share dynamics**

The neocloud market (specialist GPU-as-a-service providers) is dominated by CoreWeave, Nebius, Lambda Labs, and a handful of smaller operators. Nebius's $46.4B in contracted revenue (Meta + Microsoft) gives it among the largest known contracted order books in the sector. Market share within the neocloud segment is not uniformly published, but Nebius's geographic positioning (US + Europe + Israel sovereign) across multiple jurisdictions provides diversification that single-geography competitors lack.

**6. Displacement risk**

The most credible displacement scenario is hyperscaler insourcing. Microsoft is building its own data centres and owns equity in OpenAI infrastructure. The $19.4B Nebius contract suggests Microsoft is buying external capacity to supplement, not replace, its own build. This is a structural outsourcing play — hyperscalers cannot build fast enough internally to meet their own AI training demand. That constraint is a 5–7 year structural feature, not a 12-month risk.

**OOM Test Verdict: PRIMARY ENABLER**

Without Nebius's contracted capacity, Meta and Microsoft cannot close their own AI training compute gaps in the 2026–2028 window. The contracts are evidence of a genuine supply gap. Nebius is not peripheral.

## SECTION 1 — WHICH LAYER OF THE TRILLION-DOLLAR CLUSTER?

**Layer O (Cloud/GPU-as-a-Service) + Layer F (Physical Fabric)**

**Why Layer O is non-optional at scale:**

Running 1M GPUs at full utilisation requires approximately 100–150 MW of power per 10,000 GPUs — roughly 10–15 GW for a 1M GPU cluster. No single hyperscaler owns that capacity today. The shortfall must be outsourced. At the 2026 cluster scale (1M GPUs / 1 GW), the physics force hyperscalers to buy capacity from dedicated GPU cloud providers. Nebius's contracted book of 3.5+ GW is direct evidence that this demand is real and pre-committed.

**Competitive landscape in Layer O:**

- **CoreWeave** — largest US neocloud; deep Microsoft relationship; US-centric geography
- **Lambda Labs** — strong developer positioning; smaller balance sheet
- **Nebius Group** — strongest European + allied sovereign positioning; largest disclosed contracted revenue; NVIDIA strategic investor; Israeli national supercomputer operator

Nebius's differentiation: the only major neocloud with a sovereign government infrastructure contract (Israel), European GDPR-compliant positioning, and a joint NVIDIA investment underpinning GPU supply priority.

**Layer O bottleneck evidence:**

Q1 2026 management language: "multiple customers compete for every GPU brought online." The pipeline grew 3.5× QoQ. CapEx was raised in response to pre-committed demand, not speculative demand.

**How Layer O scales with cluster growth:**

GPU compute demand scales super-linearly with AI model size (scaling laws). The 100k→1M GPU transition (2024→2026) requires 10× the compute; the 1M→10M GPU transition (2026→2028) requires another 10×. Nebius's contracted pipeline of 4+ GW is positioned precisely at the 100k→1M GPU transition. The 2028 transition to 10 GW is what the Missouri 1.2 GW campus and the continued Finland buildout address.

**Government / national security argument for Layer O:**

Israel's National AI Supercomputer — operational as of early 2026, using NVIDIA Blackwell B200 accelerators — is a directly confirmed government contract. The Directorate of Defence R&D (MAFAT) is a co-funding stakeholder. This establishes Nebius as the first major neocloud operator with an active defence-adjacent government contract for AI compute.

**Score: 1/1 — Strong**
