## SECTION 1 — WHICH LAYER OF THE TRILLION-DOLLAR CLUSTER?

1.  **Layer classification.** Layer P — Power & Energy.
2.  **Why this layer is non-optional.** Running a 1M GPU cluster requires 1 GW of continuous baseload power. Traditional electrical grids face transmission and transformer bottlenecks, with interconnection wait times in regions like Northern Virginia (Dominion) and the PJM Interconnection stretching out to 2029–2032. On-site power generation is non-optional to meet the rapid deployment schedules of AI developers.
3.  **Primary provider vs. competitors.** Bloom is the primary provider of high-capacity solid oxide fuel cells. Direct fuel cell competitors, such as FuelCell Energy and Ballard Power, rely on molten carbonate or proton-exchange membrane technologies, which either have lower electrical efficiency or require pure hydrogen feedstock, making them unviable for continuous, natural-gas-driven data centre baseloads.
4.  **Bottleneck status.** Yes, the power layer is the single largest physical bottleneck in the AI buildout. Lead times for utility grid upgrades exceed 48 months, whereas Bloom's containerised Energy Servers can be deployed in under 12 months.
5.  **Government and national security argument.** Yes, on-site microgrids provide physical energy security and isolation for sovereign clouds and classified defense installations (e.g., NSA Fort Meade), securing a structural demand floor.
