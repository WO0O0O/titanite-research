## SECTION 0 — THE OOM TEST

**1. Current revenue per cluster scale (~100k GPUs / 100MW):**
At the 2024 cluster scale, Rubrik generated $1.316 billion in FY26 revenue (ended January 2026). Subscription ARR was $1.46 billion. The vast majority of this revenue was derived from traditional enterprise data protection (backup, recovery, and threat monitoring), with minimal direct contribution from AI-specific workloads.

**2. Revenue at 1M GPUs / 1GW (2026 cluster scale):**
At the 1M GPU scale, Rubrik's Subscription ARR is guided to $1.854B–$1.862B (end-FY27). Revenue scales sub-linearly with cluster size because Rubrik is priced per protected workload or capacity, rather than scaling directly with GPU deployments. However, the launch of Rubrik Agent Cloud in 2026 begins to link revenue to the volume of deployed autonomous AI agents.

**3. Revenue at 10M GPUs / 10GW (2028 cluster scale):**
At the 10M GPU scale, Rubrik remains relevant. As enterprises deploy autonomous agent networks, the risk of data corruption from rogue agents mandates a dedicated safety net. Rubrik's "Agent Rewind" capability provides this. If adopted widely, Subscription ARR scales to ~$2.80 billion by FY28.

**4. Revenue at 100M GPUs / 100GW (2030 cluster scale):**
At this scale, the key risk is **technology obsolescence**. If next-generation AGI systems incorporate native, self-healing data layers, the requirement for independent backup and restore platforms diminishes. Additionally, public cloud providers (Microsoft, AWS) may build native, zero-cost agent governance tools, bypassing Rubrik entirely.

**5. Market share dynamics:**
Rubrik holds a leading position in the modern data protection and cyber resilience market, alongside Cohesity. The market is consolidate-stable, with legacy vendors (Veritas, Commvault) losing share to modern platforms.

**6. Displacement risk:**
The most credible threat is vertical integration by hyperscalers. Microsoft Azure could integrate advanced backup and recovery tools directly into the Azure platform at lower cost. However, Rubrik's multi-cloud capability (protecting data across Azure, AWS, GCP, and on-premises simultaneously) provides a persistent moat.

**OOM Test Verdict: SCALED BENEFICIARY**
Rubrik is not a primary enabler; the trillion-dollar cluster can be constructed and operated without Rubrik's software. However, Rubrik is a scaled beneficiary, as the massive data volumes and agentic workflows generated by the buildout expand its addressable market and drive adoption of its security cloud.

## SYNTHESIS: THE ONE-PARAGRAPH PITCH

Rubrik operates at the intersection of Layer S (Software & Orchestration) and Layer G (Government & Sovereign) as a scaled beneficiary of the AI infrastructure buildout. Its Rubrik Security Cloud protects critical enterprise data assets, whilst the newly launched Rubrik Agent Cloud introduces a critical governance layer to manage autonomous AI agent fleets, supported by a Claude Mythos vulnerability patching partnership with Anthropic. The company is deeply integrated with Microsoft Azure and holds a FedRAMP Moderate Authorization sponsored by the NNSA. With $1.75 billion in cash, a net cash position of ~$650 million, and a guided fiscal year 2027 free cash flow of ~$300 million, Rubrik possesses the capital structure to support its growth without dilutive financing. However, consensus model expectations for fiscal year 2028 revenue of ~$2.0 billion are reasonably aligned, representing a moderate 1.25× consensus gap. At a $15.7 billion market capitalisation, achieving the 3× return hurdle requires Subscription ARR growth to accelerate to a 55% CAGR over 36 months, which is only plausible under a hyper-bull scenario of universal Agent Cloud adoption. Rubrik is a premium cyber resilience asset, but current pricing mandates a Tier 2 tracking position rather than a high-conviction primary holding.
