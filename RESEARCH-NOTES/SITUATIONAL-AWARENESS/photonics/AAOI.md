## SECTION 0 — THE OOM TEST

**1. Revenue at 2024 cluster scale (~100k GPUs / 100MW)**
At the 2024 cluster scale, AAOI generated minimal AI-related revenue. Full-year 2024 revenue was $249.4 million, dominated by legacy CATV and 100G data center products. AI infrastructure demand was in its infancy.

**2. Revenue at 1M GPUs / 1GW (2026 cluster scale)**
At 1 GW connected, the demand for 800G transceivers scales rapidly. In Q1 2026, AAOI's data center revenue grew 154% YoY to $81.4 million. Full-year 2026 revenue is guided to exceed $1.1 billion, representing a 2.4× increase from the 2025 base. Revenue scales super-linearly with cluster size because modern topologies require six transceivers per GPU, multiplying volume requirements.

**3. Revenue at 10M GPUs / 10GW (2028 cluster scale)**
At the 10M GPU scale, networking bandwidth requirements mandate 1.6T and 3.2T transceivers. AAOI remains critical. The company's plan to triple Texas laser capacity by mid-2027 and target over 930,000 units/month in capacity ensures it scales with the physical requirements of the 2028 cluster.

**4. Revenue at 100M GPUs / 100GW (2030 cluster scale)**
The primary risk at this scale is technology displacement by **Co-Packaged Optics (CPO)** or silicon photonics integration on-package, which would bypass pluggable transceivers. AAOI's vertical integration of laser chips protects its revenue, as CPO architectures still require external laser sources (ELS).

**5. Market share dynamics**
AAOI holds a mid-single-digit share of the global optical transceiver market, which is dominated by Chinese suppliers (Innolight, Eoptolink). However, within the Western-manufactured market segment, AAOI's market share is expanding rapidly due to its U.S.-based manufacturing capacity in Sugar Land, Texas.

**6. Displacement risk**
Displacement by a new Western entrant is limited by the high capital intensity and technical complexity of EML laser fabrication. Hyperscalers cannot easily build EML fabs in-house, creating a multi-year switching cost moat.

**OOM Test Verdict: PRIMARY ENABLER**

Without AAOI's U.S.-fabricated laser components and transceivers, Western cloud operators cannot secure sufficient, geopolitically compliant optical interconnects for their 1M and 10M GPU clusters.

## SECTION 1 — WHICH LAYER OF THE TRILLION-DOLLAR CLUSTER?

**Layer N (Networking) + Layer C (Compute Hardware / lasers)**

**Why Layer N is non-optional at scale:**
At the 1M GPU scale, electrical interconnects (copper) fail over distances greater than a few metres due to signal attenuation. High-speed optical transceivers are mandatory to link GPU nodes to leaf/spine switches across the cluster fabric. The physical requirement for transceivers scales super-linearly (6:1 ratio) because the number of ports on leaf, spine, and core switches increases exponentially to maintain a non-blocking network topology.

**Competitive landscape in Layer N:**
- **Innolight / Eoptolink** — Chinese giants; ~60% combined market share; low cost; high geopolitical risk.
- **Coherent / Lumentum** — Western leaders; strong technology; primary competitors.
- **AAOI** — Vertically integrated; unique U.S.-based manufacturing; rapid capacity scaling.

**Layer N bottleneck evidence:**
EML laser chips are in severe shortage. In the Q1 2026 call, management noted that lead times for lasers from certain suppliers are at least one year. AAOI's self-sufficiency in EML lasers bypasses this chokepoint.

**Score: 1/1 — Strong**
