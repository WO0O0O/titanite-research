## SECTION 0 — THE OOM TEST

**1. Revenue at 2024 cluster scale (~100k GPUs / 100MW)**
At the 2024 cluster scale, Marvell's data center revenue was approximately $1.4 billion to $1.8 billion, representing a moderate portion of the business as storage and optical components began their AI transition.

**2. Revenue at 1M GPUs / 1GW (2026 cluster scale)**
At 1 GW connected, the demand for 800G and 1.6T electro-optics increases. Marvell's Q1 FY2027 data center revenue reached $1.833 billion, up 27% YoY and accounting for 76% of total revenue. Full-year FY2027 revenue is guided to $11.5 billion. Revenue scales super-linearly with cluster size due to the high ratio of optical interconnects required to link massive numbers of nodes.

**3. Revenue at 10M GPUs / 10GW (2028 cluster scale)**
At the 10M GPU scale, network bandwidth limits dictate the transition to 1.6T and 3.2T optical architectures. Marvell's custom silicon programs (reaffirmed to target over $10.0 billion by FY2029) and NVLink Fusion compatibility ensure Marvell scales with the physical requirements of 10 GW clusters.

**4. Revenue at 100M GPUs / 100GW (2030 cluster scale)**
The primary risk at this scale is optical integration directly onto the compute package, bypassing separate transceivers. Marvell's acquisition of Celestial AI secures the "Photonic Fabric" optical interconnect technology, protecting the company from technology skip risk by positioning it as the leading provider of on-package optical interconnects.

**5. Market share dynamics**
Marvell holds a near-monopoly (>80% market share) in optical PAM4 DSPs and is one of two dominant custom ASIC design partners alongside Broadcom.

**6. Displacement risk**
Displacement by a new entrant is prevented by the high IP barrier of high-speed analog/mixed-signal design and the deep software integration required for custom ASICs. NVLink Fusion locks customers into the joint Nvidia-Marvell ecosystem.

**OOM Test Verdict: PRIMARY ENABLER**

Without Marvell's electro-optics and custom silicon design capabilities, hyperscalers cannot build or connect the physical nodes required for 1M and 10M GPU clusters.

## SECTION 1 — WHICH LAYER OF THE TRILLION-DOLLAR CLUSTER?

**Layer N (Networking) + Layer C (Compute Hardware)**

**Why Layer N is non-optional at scale:**
At the 1M GPU scale, copper connections are physically limited to less than three metres. High-speed optical links are mandatory for inter-chassis and inter-rack communication. The volume of transceivers scales super-linearly because networks use multi-tier non-blocking Fat-Tree topologies, multiplying the number of optical connections per GPU.

**Competitive landscape:**
- **Broadcom** — The primary competitor. Broadcom dominates the Ethernet switching silicon market (Tomahawk/Jericho) and custom ASIC market.
- **Credo / MaxLinear** — Competitors in specific optical DSP niches, but lack Marvell's vertical scale and custom silicon capability.

**Layer N bottleneck evidence:**
Advanced packaging (CoWoS) and advanced logic wafer supply are critical bottlenecks. Marvell's $1.0 billion capacity prepayment program in FY2027 is a direct response to secure advanced packaging and wafer supply from key foundries.

**Score: 1/1 — Strong**

## SECTION 13 — ADVERSARIAL TESTING: STEEL-MAN THE BEAR CASE

**1. Thesis killer — Broadcom dominance:**
Broadcom is a larger, better-capitalised competitor. If Broadcom wins the majority of custom ASIC designs at Google and Meta while displacing Marvell in Ethernet switching, Marvell's custom silicon margins will compress, limiting cash flow for future R&D.

**2. Acquisition integration risk:**
The integration of Celestial AI ($3.25B) and XConn ($540M) involves high capital allocation. If the Photonic Fabric technology faces commercialisation delays or yields are low, Marvell will write down these assets, causing significant GAAP net losses.

**3. Concentration stress test:**
If Google (the largest custom silicon customer) transitions TPU design fully in-house or to Broadcom, Marvell's data center revenue could decline by 20% to 25%, causing the stock to re-rate lower.

**Overall Bear Case Verdict: MODERATE**

## SYNTHESIS: THE ONE-PARAGRAPH PITCH

Marvell Technology, Inc. (NASDAQ: MRVL) is a Primary Enabler in Layer N (Networking) and Layer C (Compute Hardware) of the trillion-dollar AI cluster, dominating the high-speed optical DSP market (>80% share) and serving as the custom ASIC design partner for Google, Amazon, and Meta. The company's strategic $2.0 billion partnership with NVIDIA integrates Marvell's custom XPUs and networking directly into the NVLink Fusion rack-scale fabric, securing a high-value attach rate in next-generation GPU clusters. To support this growth, Marvell is executing $1.0 billion in prepayments to TSMC to secure advanced packaging and logic wafer capacity. The consensus model underestimates this trajectory, projecting FY2028 revenue of $16.5 billion; however, the scale of optical transceivers and custom silicon at the 10M GPU cluster scale supports total revenue of $25.0 billion in the base case (a 1.51× consensus gap) and up to $34.0 billion in the bull case. Backed by $3.84 billion in cash, Deloitte audit since 2016, and the acquisitions of Celestial AI and XConn, Marvell represents a Tier 1 conviction scale-up play that anchors the optical-to-silicon transition as institutional capital rotates into Phase 3/4 advanced networking.
