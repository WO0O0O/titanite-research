## SECTION 1 — WHICH LAYER OF THE TRILLION-DOLLAR CLUSTER?

**Layer F (Physical Fabric) + Layer P (Power & Energy)**

1.  **Demand First Principles:** Running a 1M GPU Blackwell cluster requires approximately 100–120 MW of power. The heat generated by these GPUs must be removed continuously to prevent thermal throttling. At 130 kW per rack, traditional air cooling is insufficient because air cannot carry heat away fast enough. Liquid cooling (direct-to-chip water loops) is physically required, mandating coolant distribution units (CDUs) and secondary fluid loops.
2.  **Competitors:** 
    *   **Schneider Electric** — Strong in power distribution, weaker in liquid cooling.
    *   **Rittal** — Strong in industrial enclosures and liquid-cooled racks.
    *   **Eaton** — Expanding capability via the 2026 acquisition of Boyd Thermal.
    *   **Stulz** — Focuses on precision cooling, less scale in hyperscale.
3.  **Bottlenecks:** Lead times for large power and cooling systems have extended to **12 to 18 months** (compared to a legacy 9-month average), confirming that Vertiv's layer is a primary supply constraint.
4.  **Scaling Curve:** Optical networking scales super-linearly with cluster size, whereas power and cooling scale linearly with cumulative power draw. However, because rack density is rising, the dollar content per MW scales super-linearly as datacenters transition from air to liquid cooling.
5.  **National Security:** Grid power capacity and thermal management are classified as critical infrastructure. Vertiv's manufacturing base is primary U.S. and allied-based, supporting the sovereign cloud narrative.
