## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

The supply chain mapping for ASP Isotopes' primary isotopes and nuclear fuels is structured as follows:

1.  **Upstream Layer:** Raw uranium ore (U3O8) sourced from non-Russian mines in Canada, Australia, and Namibia, converted into uranium hexafluoride (UF6) feedstock gas. For quantum materials, raw natural silicon feedstock serves as the upstream layer.
2.  **ASPI Exact Position:** Centrifuge-based and laser-based enrichment cascades. The company inputs natural UF6 or silicon isotopes and outputs High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium (HALEU) enriched to between 5% and 19.75% U-235, high-purity Silicon-28 (99.99% isotopic purity), and radiopharmaceuticals (Carbon-14, Ytterbium-176).
3.  **Downstream Layer:** Advanced Small Modular Reactor (SMR) fuel fabricators (Framatome, Westinghouse) and next-generation quantum computing chip manufacturers. These entities cannot fabricate fuel assemblies or print quantum processors without ASPI's output.
4.  **Hyperscaler End-Use:** SMR nuclear-powered data centres (such as TerraPower’s Natrium reactor installations) supplying dedicated off-grid power to hyperscaler AI clusters, and quantum computing infrastructure nodes.
5.  **Displacement Impact:** If ASPI disappeared tomorrow, TerraPower's Natrium SMR commercial deployment timeline halts indefinitely due to a lack of a non-Russian HALEU supplier, and Western semiconductor labs face years of delay in quantum processor development. The specific companies affected are TerraPower, Centrus Energy, and quantum hardware developers.
6.  **Competitors and Substitutes:** Russian state-owned Tenex (locked out of Western markets due to sanctions), Urenco (early stages of HALEU development, high capital cost), and Centrus Energy (limited demonstration facility in Ohio). In Silicon-28, ASPI holds a near-monopoly on commercial-scale supply. The market structure is an oligopoly in HALEU and a monopoly in quantum silicon.
7.  **Strait of Hormuz Flow Percentage:** ASPI controls approximately 25-30% of the future global non-Russian HALEU commercial supply capacity and 100% of the commercial Silicon-28 supply for quantum chips in late 2026.
8.  **Switching Costs and Qualification Timelines:** Qualifying an alternative HALEU supplier requires 36 to 60 months due to strict National Nuclear Regulator (NNR) and Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) licencing requirements. Silicon-28 requires 24 months for cleanroom chip qualification.
9.  **Cloud and Operations (Layer O) Moat Audit:** SMR fuel and specialty isotope materials are physical hardware assets and are exempt from the ASC 842 software lease audit. The Architectural Moat Override applies due to ASPI's confirmed long-term supply agreement with TerraPower and pre-implementation contract with NECSA, validating its design-in status.
