## FRAMEWORK MODIFIER — QUALIFICATION-CYCLE PLAYERS

Centrus Energy is classified as a hybrid **qualification-cycle player**. While the legacy LEU brokerage segment generates substantial current revenues (~$448.7 million in 2025), the core investment thesis is centered on its Piketon commercial centrifuge expansion for LEU and advanced HALEU fuel, which is currently in the pre-commercial qualification phase. Mass commercial HALEU volume (12 metric tons) and full LEU cascades will not achieve scale until the late 2020s or early 2030s.

Under the framework modifier, the company is evaluated on its contract backlog, DOE task orders, centrifuge manufacturing milestones, and utility advisory commitments, rather than trailing gross profit margins, which are highly volatile due to shipment timing.

## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

1.  **Upstream Layer:** Natural uranium mining and conversion to uranium hexafluoride ($UF_6$).
2.  **Centrus Position:** Nuclear fuel supplier/broker and advanced centrifuge technology developer. Sourced enriched uranium (SWU) from global enrichers (Tenex, Orano) and is building its own centrifuge cascades in Piketon, Ohio, to enrich LEU and HALEU.
3.  **Downstream Layer:** Commercial nuclear utilities (LEU) and advanced SMR developers (HALEU).
4.  **Hyperscaler End-Use:** Clean, zero-carbon, baseload electricity grids designed to power hyperscaler artificial intelligence data centre clusters.
5.  **Disappearance Breakage:** If Centrus disappeared tomorrow, U.S. commercial nuclear utilities would face immediate fuel procurement shortages, as Centrus supplies approximately 20-25% of the domestic market. Furthermore, the entire U.S. advanced reactor/SMR development program would halt immediately, as Centrus is the only licensed domestic producer of HALEU fuel.
6.  **Competitors:** Urenco (UK/US), Orano (France), and Rosatom/Tenex (Russia) for LEU. For HALEU, Urenco (UK/US plans) and ASP Isotopes Inc. (South Africa/Iceland partnerships) are developing capacity, but Centrus is the first to achieve active U.S. production.
7.  **Strait of Hormuz Flow Percentage:** Centrus supplies approximately 20-25% of U.S. commercial LEU requirements (sourced from global suppliers). For domestic HALEU, Centrus represents 100% of current domestic production.
8.  **Switching Costs:** Extremely high. It takes 3-5 years for a utility to qualify alternative enrichment suppliers and navigate regulatory approvals. For HALEU, there are zero alternative domestic suppliers today.

**Required Verdict:** CHOKEPOINT (due to its monopoly on domestic HALEU production and critical role in U.S. nuclear fuel supply).

## SYNTHESIS: THE ONE-PARAGRAPH PITCH

Centrus Energy Corp. (LEU) is a primary enabler of the advanced nuclear fuel layer (Layer P), holding a strategic monopoly as the only U.S. company licensed by the NRC to produce High-Assay, Low-Enriched Uranium (HALEU) using proprietary American centrifuge technology. As hyperscaler AI datacenters look to small modular reactors (SMRs) to bypass grid capacity constraints, Centrus's HALEU production is non-optional for commercial reactor deployments. This is supported by a $3.9 billion commercial backlog extending to 2040 and a $900 million DOE task order awarded in January 2026. However, at a market capitalisation of $3.47 billion, the stock fails the 5.0x return hurdle over 36 months, with the valuation already discounting a portion of future commercial success. While the company's reliance on Russian low-enriched uranium (LEU) through 2027 under U.S. waivers introduces shipment friction (exacerbated by Russia's case-by-case export licenses), the structural transition to domestic enrichment makes LEU a Tier 2 investment thesis, offering a clear 2.3x return pathway as U.S.-owned cascades come online.
