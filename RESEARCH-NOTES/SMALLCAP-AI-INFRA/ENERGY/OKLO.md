## ACTIVE SHORT THESIS RECONCILIATION

Oklo was targeted by a short-seller report from **Kerrisdale Capital (20 November 2024)** titled *"Fission Impossible"*. The core allegations and their current status as of June 2026 are summarised below:

1.  **Regulatory and Commercial Viability:** Kerrisdale asserted that Oklo lacks a regulator-approved design and that its 15–50 MWe Aurora microreactor has no proven commercial viability. They argued that a late 2027 deployment timeline was unrealistic, citing former NRC commissioner opinions that licensing would require at least four years.
    *   *Reconciliation:* Oklo remains pre-commercial, and the timeline to first commercial cash flow is years away. However, on 6 May 2026, the NRC approved Oklo's Principal Design Criteria (PDC) topical report on an accelerated review schedule. This establishes a foundational regulatory framework that significantly reduces licensing risk.
2.  **Financial and Capital Constraints:** The report argued that Oklo had sold the market on inflated unit economics, underestimating the capital needed to commercialise, and would require billions in dilutive funding.
    *   *Reconciliation:* Oklo has resolved its near-term capital constraints. In Q1 2026, the company completed a massive ATM equity program, raising approximately $1.18 billion in net proceeds. Oklo ended Q1 2026 with $2.54 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities, with zero debt. While the raise was dilutive, it fully funds its projected development and capital expenditure through the late 2020s.
3.  **Management and Operational Inexperience:** Kerrisdale criticised the management team as inexperienced and noted its small employee headcount relative to competitors.
    *   *Reconciliation:* Co-founder and CEO Jacob DeWitte holds a PhD in nuclear engineering from MIT and has spent over a decade developing the Aurora design. CFO Craig Bealmear (former BP CFO) brings deep experience managing multi-billion dollar capital programs.

Following the report, several law firms initiated shareholder investigations, but no active SEC or regulatory enforcement actions have materialised against the company or its executives.
