## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

### Supply Chain Map: BaTiO3 → MLCC → AI Server

```
[Layer 1 — Raw Minerals]
Barium carbonate (BaCO3) + Titanium dioxide (TiO2)
→ Sourced from global chemical commodity markets
→ NCI self-manufactures high-purity BaCO3 (upstream integration point)

[Layer 2 — NCI's Position ← HERE]
HIGH-PURITY BARIUM TITANATE (BaTiO3)
• Oxalate method → high Ba/Ti ratio precision
• Alkoxide method → nano-scale particles for next-gen thin layers
• Brands: BESPA, Pulceram
• Takes in: BaCO3 + TiO2
• Produces: Electronic-grade BaTiO3 powder (D50: 100–500nm)

[Layer 3 — MLCC Manufacturers (direct customers)]
TDK, Murata, Samsung Electro-Mechanics, Taiyo Yuden, Yageo
→ Mix BaTiO3 with binders, print ceramic green sheets, sinter
→ Produce MLCCs: 0402, 0201, 01005 sizes for AI/auto

[Layer 4 — AI Server ODM/OEM]
Foxconn, Quanta, Wistron, Supermicro
→ Assemble AI server motherboards
→ Each server: 10,000–30,000 MLCCs for GPU decoupling/power delivery

[Layer 5 — Hyperscaler End-Use]
NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 racks → AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud, Meta, Oracle
→ AI training/inference infrastructure
```

### Chokepoint Analysis

| Question                                      | Answer                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| --------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Who cannot ship without NCI?**              | TDK (confirmed via JV); indirectly Murata (through competitive allocation)                                                                                                                   |
| **If NCI disappeared tomorrow, what breaks?** | TDK's high-end BaTiO3 supply is disrupted within 1–2 quarters; qualification of alternative source would take 6–18 months given the precision chemistry required                             |
| **Every known competitor**                    | Sakai Chemical Industry (Japan, #1 globally), Fuji Titanium/MF Material JV (Murata-backed), Vibrantz Technologies (US/global), Merck KGaA (Germany), Shandong Sinocera (China, fast-growing) |
| **Market structure**                          | Oligopoly — top 5–6 producers control ~60–70% of premium electronic-grade BaTiO3                                                                                                             |
| **Strait of Hormuz %**                        | NCI estimated at **8–15% of global high-purity, electronic-grade BaTiO3** (not dominant, but material). Not a monopoly — this is key.                                                        |
| **Switching cost / qualification time**       | 6–18 months for an MLCC manufacturer to qualify a new BaTiO3 supplier for a given product line; ongoing re-qualification per product generation                                              |
| **China exposure**                            | Shandong Sinocera is a rapidly scaling Chinese competitor — structural risk to NCI's commodity-adjacent grades. High-precision nano grades remain defensible for now.                        |

### **Verdict: PARTIAL CHOKEPOINT**

NCI is **not** the Strait of Hormuz in isolation — Sakai Chemical is larger and Sinocera is growing. However, NCI occupies a **confirmed, defensible oligopoly position** in the electronic-grade BaTiO3 segment. Its strategic lock-in is reinforced by the TDK JV, which effectively converts TDK (the world's #2 MLCC maker) into a co-development partner. That is a _partial_ but real chokepoint in a structurally constrained market.

> ⚠️ **The thesis does NOT stop here** under the partial chokepoint ruling — but scoring must reflect the oligopoly-not-monopoly reality.

## SECTION 1 — WHICH AI INFRA BOTTLENECK DOES IT SOLVE?

**Score: 1/1**

**Bottleneck addressed:** Substrates and materials — specifically, the dielectric ceramic powder that is the functional core of every MLCC.

**Why MLCCs are the bottleneck:**

- NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 rack: estimated 1.5–2 million MLCCs per rack, primarily high-capacitance types for GPU power delivery and decoupling
- High-capacitance MLCCs (>10µF, >100V, X5R/X7R dielectric) require the highest-purity, most precisely controlled BaTiO3
- AI server MLCC demand is projected to grow at >20% CAGR through 2028; the constraint is not the assembler — it's the ceramic materials

**Why NCI is a primary solver:**

- One of only 4–5 companies globally that produce precision electronic-grade BaTiO3 at commercial scale in non-China jurisdictions
- The TDK JV is explicitly created to solve next-generation MLCC ceramic material development for AI-class components
- NCI's alkoxide method produces nano-scale particles (<150nm) necessary for next-generation ultra-thin-layer MLCCs (layers <0.5µm) that deliver higher capacitance in the same footprint

**Hyperscalers affected if BaTiO3 supply constricts:** AWS, Google, Microsoft, Meta — all dependent on Murata/TDK/Samsung MLCC output which in turn depends on NCI-grade materials.

**Score: 1/1 — bottleneck is specific, quantifiable, NCI is a material solver (not peripheral).**

- **Merck KGaA**: Niche high-purity supplier — limited volume threat.

**Assessment:** MF Material is a 2027 capacity expansion, not yet in market at scale. NCI has 12–24 months of competitive runway before MF Material represents a genuine volume threat to TDK relationship.

### 4. Concentration Stress Test

## SYNTHESIS: THE ONE-PARAGRAPH PITCH

**Nippon Chemical Industrial (TSE: 4092, ~$235M market cap) is a partial chokepoint in the deepest layer of the AI server supply chain** — one of only 4–5 non-Chinese producers of precision electronic-grade barium titanate (BaTiO3), the ceramic dielectric that is the functional heart of every high-capacitance MLCC. A single GB200 NVL72 AI rack requires an estimated 1.5–2M MLCCs for GPU decoupling; those MLCCs flow through TDK, Murata, and Samsung Electro-Mechanics — all of whom source from NCI-class suppliers. On April 1, 2026, TDK (world's #2 MLCC maker) created a joint venture with NCI specifically to accelerate next-generation ceramic materials development — TDK does not form JVs with commodity suppliers. High-end MLCC lead times have extended beyond 20 weeks; AI-grade MLCC prices are up 15–35%; and NCI's electronic ceramics segment is growing while blended operating margins are depressed by an unrelated battery materials drag — the market is penalising the wrong number. FY2027 operating profit guidance is +15.9% on only +1.5% revenue growth, signalling a mix shift to higher-margin AI materials. The stock has re-rated +89–147% over the past 12 months but still trades at 0.85x P/S and 0.55x P/B — no global institutional coverage, no English-language analyst initiation, buried under a "legacy chemicals" classification. Bull-case 24–36 month target: ¥100–150B market cap ($670–$1B USD) on a P/S re-rate to 2–2.5x as AI-materials classification takes hold — implying 3–4.3x from current levels, below the framework's 5x threshold and the primary reason this is a **Tier 2 rather than Tier 1** conviction. Key catalysts: TDK JV first commercial shipment announcement; electronic ceramics segment exceeding 40% of revenue; first global analyst initiation with AI-materials framing. Bear case is MODERATE — Murata's competing BaTiO3 JV (MF Material, 2027 expansion) and Sinocera's scaling are real threats, but NCI's TDK co-development relationship creates a structural switching cost that neither can replicate quickly. No active short thesis. No integrity issues. Position sizing: **partial now, add aggressively on any of the three named catalysts.**
