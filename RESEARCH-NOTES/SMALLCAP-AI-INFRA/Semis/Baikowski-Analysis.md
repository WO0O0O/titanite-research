## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

### 1. Upstream Layer

The upstream layer consists of high-grade raw aluminum metal (ingots), aluminum ammonium sulfate (ammonium alum), and bauxite/Bayer process hydrates. These raw materials are commodities sourced from global producers.

### 2. Baikowski's Exact Position

Baikowski processes chemical precursors (alum, alkoxides, hydrates) through chemical synthesis, purification, and high-temperature calcination. The company outputs ultra-pure alumina powders (99.99% / 4N and 99.999% / 5N purity), custom chemical formulations, and polishing slurries.

### 3. Downstream Layer

The downstream layer comprises semiconductor CMP slurry formulators (Fujifilm, Merck KGaA, Resonac, Entegris), lithium-ion battery separator coaters (Asahi Kasei, Toray, SK IE Technology), and aerospace engine OEMs (Safran, GE Aerospace, Rolls-Royce).

### 4. Hyperscaler End-Use

The polished silicon wafers and advanced packages (such as TSMC's CoWoS) are used to manufacture cutting-edge AI accelerators (NVIDIA Blackwell, AMD MI300) and SiC/GaN power chips for high-efficiency AI data centre power supplies. Aerospace CMCs are deployed in commercial and military aviation engines.

### 5. Disappearance Impact

High. If Baikowski disappeared, global semiconductor wafer polishing would face immediate disruptions. Re-qualifying alternative alumina powders from Sumitomo or Nippon Light Metal requires 2 to 5 years of testing. During this period, semiconductor fabs would experience elevated defect rates and yield losses. Aerospace CMC production lines would halt due to a lack of certified mullite and alumina matrix powders.

### 6. Competitors and Substitutes

- **Sumitomo Chemical (Japan):** The dominant global leader in 5N/6N HPA (estimated 7.5% total market share), using alkoxide hydrolysis.
- **Nippon Light Metal (Japan):** Major supplier of high-grade HPA to Asian semiconductor fabs.
- **Sasol (South Africa):** High-volume supplier of chemical-grade HPA.
- **Alpha HPA (Australia):** Emerging competitor scaling low-carbon HPA production.
- **Oligopoly:** The market for ultra-pure (4N/5N) customized alumina is a tight oligopoly.

### 7. Strait of Hormuz Market Share

Baikowski controls **~19%** of the global High Purity Alumina (HPA) market and **20% to 25%** of the global alumina-based precision polishing suspensions segment.

### 8. Switching Costs

Extremely high. Re-certifying a new alumina powder supplier takes 2 to 5 years for semiconductor CMP slurries and jet engine CMCs due to the specific crystal structure, morphology, and chemical modifications required for client machines.

### **Verdict: PARTIAL CHOKEPOINT**

Baikowski SA operates as a partial chokepoint in the semiconductor CMP slurry and aerospace CMC supply chains due to its high market share and multi-year customer qualification cycles.

## SECTION 1 — WHICH AI INFRA BOTTLENECK DOES IT SOLVE?

- **Bottleneck:** Semiconductor Materials (Chemical Mechanical Planarization slurries for advanced packaging and sub-3nm nodes) and Data Center Power (SiC/GaN power conversion chips).
- **Analysis:** Modern microchips require Angstrom-level flatness across wafer layers. Alumina CMP slurries are the primary chemistry used to polish hard materials like silicon carbide (SiC), gallium nitride (GaN), and sapphire. The transition to advanced packaging (CoWoS) and SiC-based power supplies for 100kW+ AI server racks increases the volume of CMP steps per wafer. Baikowski's ultra-pure (5N) alumina powders prevent nanoscale defects that ruin entire wafer batches.
- **Quantifiable Impact:** Baikowski's CMP and electronics revenue grew in 2025, driven by the expansion of semiconductor fabs in Asia and the US.

### **Score: 1/1**

Baikowski is a primary supplier of the ultra-pure alumina powders required for high-precision semiconductor CMP slurries.

## SECTION 13 — ADVERSARIAL TESTING: STEEL-MAN THE BEAR CASE

### 1. The Thesis Killer

The primary threat is the rapid commercialization of low-cost, high-purity alumina from emerging producers using alternative methods (such as hydrochloric acid leaching from kaolin clay, e.g., Alpha HPA) that bypass the high-cost alum or alkoxide routes. If these competitors can match Baikowski's customization capabilities at a 30% lower cost, Baikowski's pricing power and 65% gross margins will compress.

### 2. Substitute Threat

Alternative abrasive materials (such as ceria or colloidal silica) could replace alumina in next-generation CMP slurry formulations for specific semiconductor nodes, reducing the addressable market.

### 3. Customer Concentration Stress Test

The top 10 customers account for 60% of revenue. If a key semiconductor slurry manufacturer (representing ~10% of revenue) switches to a competitor, revenue would decline by €5.0 million, and EBITDA would contract by approximately €1.5 million.

### 4. Balance Sheet and Runway Analysis

- **Cash Position:** €8.10 million.
- **Net Debt:** €8.40 million.
- **Runway:** High. The company is cash-flow positive and faces no near-term debt maturity risks.

### **Bear Case Rating: MODERATE**

While competitor scaling and customer concentration are risks, the multi-year customer qualification cycles and customization lock-in provide a strong barrier to entry.
