## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

### 1. Upstream Layer
IQE purchases raw compound semiconductor substrates—primarily Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) and Indium Phosphide (InP) wafers—along with specialty precursor chemicals (trimethylgallium, trimethylindium) and high-purity gases. Key substrate suppliers include Sumitomo Electric and AXT.

### 2. IQE's Exact Position
IQE operates as an outsourced epitaxial wafer (epi-wafer) manufacturer. It takes in raw substrates and grows thin, atomically-precise crystalline layers on top using Metal Organic Chemical Vapor Deposition (MOCVD) and Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE). It does not design or fabricate finished chips; it sells the processed epi-wafers downstream.

### 3. Downstream Layer
Epi-wafers are shipped to optoelectronic device manufacturers, foundries, and integrated device manufacturers (IDMs), including Coherent, Lumentum, Win Semiconductors, and now MACOM. These customers etch, metallise, and package the wafers into optical components (VCSELs, EMLs, photodetectors) and RF transceivers.

### 4. Hyperscaler End-Use
The downstream components (lasers and detectors) are integrated into 800G and 1.6T optical transceivers. These transceivers are purchased by hyperscalers (Meta, Microsoft, Google, AWS) to connect GPUs within AI clusters (e.g., NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 architectures).

### 5. Disappearance Impact
If IQE disappeared, the merchant supply of advanced compound semiconductor epi-wafers would halt. Downstream manufacturers like Coherent and Lumentum would lose immediate access to over 50% of their outsourced wafer supply. Because internal epitaxy lines at these companies are running at high capacity, they would be unable to absorb the volume. Re-qualifying a new merchant epi-supplier takes 12 to 18 months, causing a severe bottleneck in the global supply of 800G/1.6T optical transceivers.

### 6. Competitors and Substitutes
*   **VPEC (Visual Photonics Epitaxy Co.):** Principal Taiwanese competitor in GaAs/InP epi-wafers.
*   **Landmark Optoelectronics:** Focused on optoelectronic epi-growth in Taiwan.
*   **IntelliEPI:** US-based pure-play MBE supplier (focus on RF and InP detectors).
*   **In-house/Captive Epi:** Coherent and Lumentum maintain internal MOCVD/MBE lines but lack the scale to support their total market requirements without merchant outsourcing.

### 7. Strait of Hormuz Market Share
IQE controls **over 50%** of the global outsourced merchant market for advanced compound semiconductor epitaxial wafers. In GaAs-based RF and InP-based optical structures, its share of the open merchant market exceeds 55%.

### 8. Switching Costs
High. Qualifying an alternative epi-wafer supplier requires replicating exact crystalline growth recipes on MOCVD reactors. The qualification process with major optical transceivers and network equipment manufacturers requires 12 to 18 months of reliability testing.

### **Verdict: PARTIAL CHOKEPOINT**
While internal epi-wafer capacity exists among large IDMs, the merchant transceiver ecosystem is structurally dependent on IQE to meet volume demands. IQE is a partial chokepoint in the optical interconnect supply chain.

## SECTION 1 — WHICH AI INFRA BOTTLENECK DOES IT SOLVE?

*   **Primary Bottleneck:** Optical Interconnect.
*   **Technical Details:** High-density AI training clusters are hitting the physical limits of copper wiring for GPU-to-GPU data transmission due to electrical attenuation and high thermal dissipation. Optical interconnects are the only viable path to achieve the bandwidth required for 800G and 1.6T networks. 
*   **IQE's Role:** These optical links require lasers—specifically Indium Phosphide (InP) Edge Emitting Lasers (EMLs) and Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) Vertical-Cavity Surface-Emitting Lasers (VCSELs). IQE is the primary merchant supplier of the InP and GaAs epi-wafers required to build these lasers.
*   **Quantifiable Impact:** In FY 2025, IQE's Photonics division grew 15% to £57.1 million, driven directly by AI data centre demands and US military programmes.

### **Score: 1/1**
IQE is a primary supplier of the material foundations solving the physical optical bandwidth limit in AI data centres.

## SECTION 8 — TECHNOLOGY LEADERSHIP / FIRST-MOVER ADVANTAGE

*   **Technical Moat:** Process IP in MOCVD deposition. Growing uniform, defect-free atomic crystal layers across 6-inch and 8-inch compound semiconductor wafers requires precise thermal management and precursor chemistry control. IQE holds a portfolio of over 150 patent families covering epitaxial layer designs and compound-on-silicon integration.
*   **Porous Silicon IP:** IQE's proprietary "porous silicon" technology provides structural moats in advanced RF filters and optoelectronic integration. The technology is subject to active IP defense (e.g., the Tower Semiconductor litigation).
*   **Replication Barrier:** Replicating IQE's MOCVD reactor fleet (over 100 reactors globally) would require a capital investment exceeding £300 million and a minimum of 24 months in customer qualification cycles.

### **Score: 1/1**
IQE maintains a clear technology lead in independent merchant epi-wafer supply, protected by process IP and high capital barriers to replication.

## SECTION 13 — ADVERSARIAL TESTING: STEEL-MAN THE BEAR CASE

### 1. The Thesis Killer
The most credible risk is that silicon photonics heterogeneous integration technologies shift toward direct Silicon-on-Insulator (SOI) processes that bypass the need for outsourced compound semiconductor epitaxial layers entirely, or that large IDMs (such as Coherent) successfully expand their internal MOCVD capacity to absorb 100% of their optical transceiver demand, rendering IQE's merchant capacity redundant.

### 2. Substitute Threat
VPEC or Landmark Optoelectronics aggressively expand their InP MOCVD capacity in Taiwan, triggering a price war in advanced optical epi-wafers that compresses IQE's gross margins back to trough levels.

### 3. Customer Concentration Stress Test
If Coherent or MACOM terminated their relationship, it would remove ~20% of group revenue (£19 million). With fixed overheads at the Greensboro and Cardiff fabs, this would drive adjusted EBITDA margins back to negative territory, forcing further capital preservation measures.

### 4. Balance Sheet and Runway Analysis
*   **Cash Position:** Adjusted cash post-fundraise is estimated at ~£30 million (after paying down RCF and note redemptions).
*   **Runway:** With net debt reduced and the RCF repaid, the company has over 8 quarters of operational runway under a flat revenue scenario, and is positioned to reach free cash flow neutrality in FY 2026/2027 if the guided >20% revenue growth is achieved.

### **Bear Case Rating: MODERATE**
The balance sheet risk has been mitigated by the £81m raise, but the structural threat of customer insourcing and competitive pricing from Taiwanese competitors remains a constant margin risk.

## SYNTHESIS: THE ONE-PARAGRAPH PITCH

IQE plc holds over 55% of the outsourced merchant market for advanced compound semiconductor epitaxial wafers, creating a critical materials chokepoint for the Indium Phosphide (InP) and Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) lasers that power 800G and 1.6T optical transceivers. This hyperscaler linkage was validated in April 2026 when Tier 1 supplier MACOM executed an £81 million strategic rescue and growth package, taking an 11.5% equity stake and signing Long-Term Supply Agreements to secure advanced epi-capacity. While trailing consolidated financials show a trough in H1 2025 (with FY 2025 gross margin at 1.4% due to a 40% collapse in wireless handset volumes), the Photonics division grew 15% to £57.1 million, and management has guided for >20% revenue growth in FY 2026 as the higher-margin optical business inflects. At a £623 million market cap, the stock represents a 2.0× return target under an aggressive 36-month bull case, positioning it as an under-researched Phase 3 institutional rotation candidate prior to the wider market recognizing the structural shift from copper to optical interconnects.
