## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

1. **Upstream layer:** Raw graphite and graphene nanoplatelets, silicone polymers, and specialized alignment chemicals.
2. **SHT position:** SHT processes raw materials to manufacture GT-TIM®, a graphene-enhanced thermal interface material. SHT owns the patented process for vertical alignment of graphene sheets.
3. **Downstream layer:** Consumer retail brands (Thermal Grizzly's KryoSheet), industrial B2B distributors (Henkel selling under the Loctite brand), and processor test developers (AMD). The ultimate downstreams are AI hardware module contract manufacturers.
4. **Hyperscaler end-use:** Megawatt-scale AI training clusters using high-power GPU modules (Nvidia Blackwell and AMD MI300 series) that require advanced thermal management.
5. **Impact of SHT disappearance:** Thermal Grizzly halts KryoSheet sales immediately. AMD's factory chip testing systems lose their primary reusable thermal interface pad. Henkel's Loctite graphene TIM product line shuts down. Nvidia faces module assembly delays or must revert to multi-layer thermal interface materials or highly corrosive, conductive liquid metal.
6. **Competitors and substitutes:** Traditional thermal pastes (Shin-Etsu, Dow, Honeywell), carbon nanotube pads (CarbICE), and horizontally aligned graphite sheets (Panasonic PGS). Horizontal sheets lack vertical conductivity; SHT operates in a patented oligopoly for vertically aligned graphene films.
7. **Strait of Hormuz percentage:** SHT controls 100% of consumer graphene sheets through Thermal Grizzly. In next-generation AI hardware migrating to single-layer TIM 1.5, SHT controls 100% of the vertically aligned graphene TIM supply chain.
8. **Switching costs:** Extremely high. Henkel's qualification cycle took three years from MOU in November 2024 to strategic partner in July 2025 and approved supplier in May 2026. For Nvidia, the qualification process spans 18 to 24 months.

**Required verdict:** CHOKEPOINT

## SECTION 1 — WHICH AI INFRA BOTTLENECK DOES IT SOLVE?

SHT addresses the **Cooling Bottleneck** at the silicon level. As GPUs and CPUs scale beyond 700W toward 1,000W+, the conventional two-layer thermal interface material (TIM) stack (TIM 1 and TIM 2) cannot transfer heat fast enough, causing thermal throttling. SHT's vertically aligned GT-TIM® acts as a single-layer TIM 1.5 replacement. It provides a vertical thermal conductivity of 50–100 W/mK, compared to 1–10 W/mK for traditional pastes. Hyperscalers cannot run Blackwell clusters at peak clock speeds without this advanced heat extraction layer. SHT is the primary technology provider enabling this transition.
