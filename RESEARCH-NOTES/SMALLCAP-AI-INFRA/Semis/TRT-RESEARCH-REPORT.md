## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

1. **Upstream Layer:** Specialty component suppliers providing bare printed circuit boards (PCBs), integrated circuit (IC) sockets (Yamaichi Electronics, Enplas), heating elements, and sensors.
2. **TRT Exact Position:** Outsourced reliability testing services and equipment manufacturing. Trio-Tech designs and manufactures custom burn-in boards (BIBs), board-testing systems (BIBTEST-55), automated device loaders, and multi-channel burn-in systems (COBIS-II). It uses this equipment internally to run package-level reliability testing services at its labs in Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand, and China.
3. **Downstream Layer:** Semiconductor integrated device manufacturers (IDMs) and fabless design houses who cannot ship finished, packaged multi-chip modules or automotive control units without reliability screening.
4. **Hyperscaler End-use:** High-power AI GPUs (e.g. Nvidia DGX systems) and advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) in electric vehicles.
5. **Disappearance Impact:** Physical reliability testing of high-power GPUs and automotive semiconductors in Southeast Asia bottlenecks immediately. Unlike logical testing, thermal burn-in requires physical oven capacity and custom board layouts. Re-routing volumes to competitors would take 6 to 12 months, stalling shipments of critical AI computing chips.
6. **Competitors:** KESM Industries (Malaysian competitor in outsourced testing) and Aehr Test Systems (wafer-level burn-in equipment).
7. **Strait of Hormuz Flow:** Trio-Tech controls an estimated 30% to 40% of the outsourced package-level reliability testing and burn-in services market for automotive and high-power computing chips in the Malaysia-Singapore semiconductor corridor.
8. **Switching Costs:** High. Qualifying an alternative testing laboratory requires auditing thermal profiles, electrostatic discharge (ESD) safety protocols, and running pilot validation lots. This process takes 6 to 9 months and requires formal customer approval.
9. **Cloud & Operations (Layer O) Moat Audit:** Exempt as a physical hardware and testing services provider.
