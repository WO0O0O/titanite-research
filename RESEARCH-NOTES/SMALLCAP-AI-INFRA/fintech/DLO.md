## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST
1. **Directly upstream:** Local banking rails, card schemes (Visa, Mastercard), and regional instant payment systems (Pix in Brazil, UPI in India).
2. **dLocal's position:** Payment aggregator and gateway. dLocal accepts international merchant instructions via a single API, executes local currency settlement, and manages cross-border remittance.
3. **Directly downstream:** Global digital merchants (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon, Spotify, Uber) who sell services to consumers in emerging markets.
4. **Hyperscaler end-use:** The collection of consumer revenue for ad platforms, cloud subscriptions, and consumer retail marketplaces in non-G10 countries.
5. **If dLocal disappeared tomorrow:** Hyperscalers would face immediate disruption in collecting regional revenue. However, their physical data centres, GPU clusters, and model training capabilities would remain completely unaffected.
6. **Competitors:** EBANX, Airwallex, Stripe, local acquirers (StoneCo, PagSeguro).
7. **Strait of Hormuz flow:** Less than 1% of global cross-border payments. The market is highly competitive and fragmented.
8. **Switching costs:** Moderate. Merchants can transition to EBANX or Stripe within three to six months.

**Verdict: COMMODITY SUPPLIER**
dLocal operates as a payment utility rather than a physical supply chain bottleneck. The thesis fails the Strait of Hormuz test.
