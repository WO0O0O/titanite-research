## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

**Supply chain mapping: Heterogeneous InP-on-Silicon lasers and photodetectors**

1. **Upstream:** Indium phosphide (InP) and Indium Gallium Arsenide (InGaAs) chemical precursors and substrates, supplied by Sumitomo Chemical Advanced Technologies and AXT.
2. **ALMU's position:** Technology platform and IP holder. Growth of compound semiconductor layers directly on 300mm silicon wafers.
3. **Downstream:** Silicon photonics foundry platforms (Tower Semiconductor) and optical transceiver manufacturers.
4. **End-use:** High-speed optical transceivers (200G/400G and future 800G/1.6T) within AI data centre networks, defense sensors, and quantum computers.
5. **If ALMU disappeared tomorrow:** Transceiver manufacturers would continue to rely on traditional, smaller (3-inch or 4-inch) standalone InP substrates or complex die-bonding techniques. The transition to scalable, low-cost 300mm integrated silicon photonics would be delayed by multiple years.
6. **Competitors:** Sivers Semiconductors (InP on 4-inch InP wafers), POET Technologies (dielectric interposer hybrid packaging), Lumentum, Coherent, MACOM, and IQE.
7. **Strait of Hormuz test:** Near 0% of current global volume flow passes through Aeluma due to its pre-production commercial stage.
8. **Switching costs:** 12–24 months for optical module suppliers to qualify alternative light sources due to laser thermal profiles and reliability certifications.

**Verdict: PARTIAL CHOKEPOINT**
Aeluma owns a critical manufacturing process solution for growing III-V lasers on standard 300mm silicon lines. While they do not control current volume flow, their platform is a structural bottleneck solver for scaling silicon photonics.

## SECTION 1 — WHICH AI INFRA BOTTLENECK DOES IT SOLVE?

**Score: 1/1 | Evidence: Strong**

The bottleneck is optical interconnect density and compound semiconductor substrate scaling. Silicon photonics cannot natively generate light; III-V materials (InP) are required for continuous-wave light sources. Traditional InP substrates are small, fragile, and highly expensive, limiting production scale. Growing InP directly on 300mm (12-inch) silicon substrates allows the integration of quantum dot lasers using standard, high-volume silicon CMOS fabrication lines, removing the cost and scaling bottlenecks of traditional foundries.

## SECTION 13 — ADVERSARIAL TESTING: STEEL-MAN THE BEAR CASE

1. **Thesis killer:** Failure to achieve commercial-grade yields on Tower Semiconductor's 300mm CMOS lines. Growing III-V materials on silicon natively creates dislocation defects. If Aeluma cannot keep defect density low enough to match the reliability and lifetime of native InP lasers, the technology will be rejected by hyperscalers in favour of hybrid bonding.
2. **Substitute threat:** Intel and Ayar Labs utilise hybrid bonding of pre-fabricated III-V dies on silicon wafers. This process is commercially proven, whereas Aeluma's direct growth method remains pre-commercial.
3. **Concentration stress test:** Total loss of government R&D funding would eliminate the company's near-term revenue and strategic validation, forcing a dilutive capital raise.
4. **Balance sheet risk:** Cash burn is elevated due to R&D scaling. With $37.8 million in cash, the company has roughly 5–6 quarters of runway at current burn rates before requiring utilization of its $50.0 million ATM program.

**Bear case rating: MODERATE**
The yield and qualification risks are high, but the company's clean balance sheet and Tower partnership provide a viable commercial runway.
