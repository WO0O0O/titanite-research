# IQE plc (IQE) Research Notes

**Date:** 1 June 2026  
**Subject:** Update on FY 2025 Financial Results, Capital Restructure, U.S. Defense Moats, and Institutional Shareholdings

---

## 1. FY 2025 Financial Results (28 May 2026)

*   **Financial Performance:**
    *   **Revenue:** Recorded at £97.3 million, down 17.6% YoY compared to FY 2024 (£118.0 million). The decline reflects underutilisation of manufacturing assets and cyclical mobile handset destocking.
    *   **Adjusted EBITDA:** Decreased 60% YoY to £3.2 million (FY 2024: £8.1 million), with EBITDA margin dropping to 3.3%.
    *   **Loss Before Tax:** £37.0 million (FY 2024: loss of £36.9 million).
*   **Segment Dynamic:**
    *   **Photonics:** Revenue grew 15% YoY to £57.1 million, driven by demand for Indium Phosphide (InP) and Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) solutions in AI and data-centre optical networks, as well as US military/defence programmes.
    *   **Wireless:** Revenue fell 40% YoY to £40.1 million, reflecting cyclical inventory adjustments in the smartphone supply chain.
*   **FY 2026 Guidance:** Management guided for revenue growth exceeding 20% year-on-year for FY 2026, driven by a strong Q1 order book and H2 visibility, with adjusted EBITDA margins inflecting to a high-single digit to low double-digit level.

## 2. Capital Restructure & MACOM Partnership (28 May 2026)

*   **Fundraising Details:** IQE successfully completed its strategic fundraising, raising £81 million in gross proceeds. 
    *   **HSBC Debt Repayment:** The proceeds were used to repay the revolving credit facility (RCF) with HSBC and clear historical loan notes. This restructure removes restrictive debt covenants that had constrained the balance sheet.
*   **MACOM Strategic Investment:**
    *   **Investment Details:** MACOM Technology Solutions Inc. invested £45 million, consisting of £30 million in equity (151,515,151 new shares at 19.8 pence per share) and £15 million in zero-coupon convertible loan notes.
    *   **Ownership Stake:** Post-transaction, MACOM holds an 11.5% voting stake in IQE.
    *   **Board Representation:** Two MACOM executives—Robert Dennehy (COO) and David O'Carroll (VP)—are appointed to the IQE Board of Directors as non-executive directors.
    *   **Commercial Agreements:** Concurrent Long-Term Supply Agreements (LTSAs) for advanced epitaxial services were signed, binding MACOM's optical transceiver material needs to IQE.

## 3. Defense Supply Chain Moats and Geopolitical Context

*   **Sole Source Defense Status:** IQE operates as a qualified sole source wafer supplier to all major U.S. defense prime contractors. These contractors are legally and structurally prohibited from sourcing materials from Chinese-backed or geopolitical risk-heavy alternative suppliers such as Landmark Optoelectronics.
*   **Raytheon Partnership:** A partnership exceeding 15 years. In January 2024, RTX's Raytheon qualified IQE's Greensboro, North Carolina facility for the manufacture of epitaxial wafers used in advanced infrared (IR) sensing and imaging systems.
*   **BAE Systems Integration:** IQE has received multiple Gold Tier Supplier Awards under BAE Systems' "Partner 2 Win" program (including for performance in 2019 and 2022), reflecting high-quality MOCVD crystal growth execution.
*   **Lockheed Martin and Northrop Grumman:** IQE's InP and GaAs wafers flow indirectly into Lockheed and Northrop aerospace platforms, integrated via components supplied by MACOM and Qorvo.
*   **Indium Phosphide (InP) Executive Order:** On 2 January 2026, U.S. President Donald Trump issued a national security executive order forcing HieFo Corp (a California-based firm with Chinese backing) to divest the InP optoelectronics assets it had acquired from EMCORE Corp. This action highlights the high-priority strategic value the U.S. government places on securing allied-nation Indium Phosphide supply chains.

## 4. Optical Transceiver and Hyperscaler Linkage

*   **Lumentum Strategic Supply Agreement:** IQE extended its multi-year strategic supply agreement with Lumentum ($LITE) in December 2025. This contract secures high-volume epi-wafer supply for Lumentum's optical and sensing portfolios, which connect directly into NVIDIA's optical interconnect buildout for AI training clusters.
