## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

**Supply chain mapping: MicroLED-based optical interconnects (Neural I/O)**

1.   **Upstream:** Gallium nitride (GaN) wafers and silicon substrates. Backplane design services.
2.   **Kopin's position:** Optical transceiver and display component developer. Repurposes MicroLED pixels as optical transceivers using its proprietary bi-directional NeuralDisplay architecture.
3.   **Downstream:** Fabless developer Fabric.AI (for Neural I/O) and military prime contractors (for thermal imaging assemblies and soldier vision systems).
4.   **End-use:** AI factory GPU-to-GPU data transmission (unproven R&D stage) and military soldier headsets.
5.   **If Kopin disappeared tomorrow:** Downstream military prime contractors would experience minor shipment delays but could transition to alternative microdisplay suppliers (eMagin, Himax, Micron). The Neural I/O project would collapse, but since it is an unproven R&D concept with zero commercial deployments, nothing in current AI data centres breaks.
6.   **Competitors:** Pluggable optics and silicon photonics developers (Lumentum, Coherent, Sivers Semiconductors, POET Technologies). For display technology, competitors include eMagin (Samsung), Himax, and Sony.
7.   **Strait of Hormuz test:** Less than 1% of total global industry flow for AI optical interconnects passes through Kopin. It is an unproven, pre-volume player.
8.   **Switching costs:** High for military defence applications due to qualification cycles (12–24 months), but zero for AI infrastructure today as the technology has not been adopted.

**Verdict: COMMODITY SUPPLIER**
Kopin is a commodity display supplier for its legacy segments and an unproven, non-commercial developer for AI infrastructure. It fails the Strait of Hormuz test.

## SECTION 13 — ADVERSARIAL TESTING: STEEL-MAN THE BEAR CASE

1.   **Thesis killer:** Kopin fails to develop a functional Neural I/O chipset by the end of 2026, leading to the cancellation of the Fabric.AI contract. Concurrently, the court rejects Kopin's BlueRadios appeal, forcing the forfeiture of the $25.3 million restricted cash bond, which triggers insolvency.
2.   **Short report reconciliation:** There are no active short reports targeting KOPN in 2026. However, the bear case is built on public filings:
     - *Litigation cash lock:* The $25.3 million restricted cash is locked in an escrow account, leaving Kopin with only $34.1 million in unrestricted cash against an quarterly operating cash burn of $3–5 million.
     - *Fabric.AI credit risk:* Fabric.AI is a thinly capitalised former cryptocurrency shell. If Fabric.AI burns through its $30 million cash balance before commercialisation, it will be unable to fund further Kopin purchase orders.
3.   **Substitute threat:** Established silicon photonics foundries and transceiver manufacturers (Lumentum, Coherent, Ayar Labs) are already delivering physical hardware to hyperscalers, bypassing Kopin's MicroLED concept entirely.
4.   **Concentration stress test:** If the military prime contractor thermal contract is suspended, Kopin's product revenue falls to near-zero, resulting in immediate insolvency.
5.   **Technology skip risk:** Hyperscalers are standardising on traditional silicon photonics (InP lasers and silicon modulators) rather than MicroLED transceivers, risking complete technological isolation for Kopin.
6.   **Balance sheet risk:** With $34.1 million in unrestricted cash and a net loss of $3.8 million in Q1 2026, the company has less than 8 quarters of runway without further dilutive capital raises.
7.   **Structural vs. temporary:** The going concern and litigation risks are structural and have persisted for multiple years, impairing operational execution.
8.   **Capex cut scenario:** A 40% hyperscaler capex cut would eliminate speculative projects like Neural I/O, destroying Kopin's AI thesis.

**Bear case rating: STRONG**
The combination of liquidity constraints, severe litigation liabilities, and a lack of validated physical hardware makes the bear case overwhelming.
