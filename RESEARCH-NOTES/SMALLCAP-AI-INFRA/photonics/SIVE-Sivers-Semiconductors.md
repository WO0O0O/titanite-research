## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

**Supply chain mapping: InP DFB laser arrays for co-packaged optics**

1. **Upstream:** Indium phosphide (InP) substrates supplied by Sumitomo Electric, JX Metals, and AXT. Sivers' Glasgow facility is vertically integrated with molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) and metal-organic chemical vapour deposition (MOCVD) capabilities.
2. **Sivers' position:** Compound semiconductor foundry. Takes InP substrates in and fabricates DFB laser arrays using its proprietary InP100 etched-facet process on 4-inch wafers. Volume manufacturing is partnered with WIN Semiconductors.
3. **Downstream:** Silicon photonics transceiver modules. Sivers supplies continuous-wave (CW) DFB laser chips to Jabil (LRO transceivers), Ayar Labs (optical engines), O-Net, and Enablence.
4. **End-use:** 800G and 1.6T optical interconnects inside AI compute clusters. End-buyers are AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft, and Meta.
5. **If Sivers disappeared tomorrow:** Downstream module integrators would face a 12–24 month qualification cycle to secure alternative InP DFB laser arrays. Only Lumentum and Coherent possess comparable Western-sovereign InP foundries, and both are capacity-constrained.
6. **Competitors:** Lumentum (EML dominant), Coherent (6-inch InP scaling), MACOM (GaAs hybrid). Sivers is a specialist in multi-wavelength CW DFB arrays for CPO.
7. **Strait of Hormuz test:** Sivers does not currently pass a major percentage of global flow. It is a pre-volume player. However, its technology is a structural bottleneck. InP DFB laser manufacturing yields are naturally 40%–70%, making qualified foundries highly scarce.
8. **Switching costs:** 12–24 months for hyperscalers to qualify alternative InP DFB laser arrays due to epitaxy variations and thermal profiling.

**Verdict: PARTIAL CHOKEPOINT**
Sivers' vertical InP fabrication capability is a structural node in the CPO supply chain. While Lumentum and Coherent are larger, Sivers' InP100 platform is qualified into key optical engines (Ayar Labs, Jabil), positioning it as a bottleneck owner as these platforms scale.

## SECTION 1 — WHICH AI INFRA BOTTLENECK DOES IT SOLVE?

**Score: 1/1 | Evidence: Strong**

The bottleneck is optical interconnect — specifically, the external light source problem for co-packaged optics. Silicon photonics integrates passive optical functions (routing, modulation) on silicon chips but cannot generate light. InP DFB laser arrays are the only commercially viable III-V solution for providing the continuous-wave light source. 

The naturally low yield of InP arrays (40%–70% bare die yield) actually intensifies the chokepoint nature of the technology, as qualified optical sources are extremely scarce.

**Score: 1**
