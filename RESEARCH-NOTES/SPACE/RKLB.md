### Step B — Fraud, Short Seller, and SPAC-Stigma Sweep
*   **Short Seller & Fraud Allegations:** On 25 February 2025, Bleecker Street Research published a short report titled *"Rocket Lab (RKLB): We Think It's Gonna Be a Long, Long Time."* The report alleged that Rocket Lab misled investors regarding the launch timeline of the Neutron rocket (claiming it was delayed to mid-2026 or 2027 vs. the company's targeted mid-2025 debut), that engine production and launch pad water systems faced major delays, and that its E-Space launch contract was a heavily discounted deal.
*   **Subsequent Impact:** Rocket Lab's stock fell 10% on the report, triggering a securities class-action lawsuit. However, the company subsequently resolved its technical issues, transitioned to automated fibre placement for tank manufacturing, and officially scheduled the first Neutron flight for Q4 2026.
*   **SPAC History & Sponsor Audit:** Rocket Lab went public via a SPAC merger with Vector Acquisition Corporation in August 2021. The pre-merger investor presentation projected 2024 revenue of $656 million and positive Adjusted EBITDA by 2024. Actual revenue was $244.6 million in 2023 and $427 million in 2024. Despite missing these initial promotional targets, the business has successfully pivoted to a prime contractor role, inflecting to a record $2.2 billion backlog and projected FY2026 revenue of ~$933.8 million.
*   **Management History:** Sir Peter Beck (CEO) and Adam Spice (CFO) have strong operational credentials. Peter Beck founded the company in 2006 and is a highly respected aerospace pioneer. There is no history of management fraud, bankruptcies, or promoter failures.
