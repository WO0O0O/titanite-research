## WRITING STYLE — MANDATORY

Apply these rules to every word of the output. Do not deviate.

**Voice and register.** Write like a serious analyst who has thought about this harder than anyone else in the room. Think Leopold Aschenbrenner's _Situational Awareness_ essays crossed with Serenity's X thread style: dense with data, zero throat-clearing, no hedging language, no caveats that exist only to protect the writer. Every sentence should carry weight. Cut anything that does not add information.

**British English throughout.** Colour, behaviour, organise, licence (noun), recognise, programme, defence, centre, catalogue. Not American spellings. Use the Oxford comma.

**No emojis. Ever.** Do not use tick marks, cross marks, or any Unicode symbol as a substitute for prose judgment. If something is good, say it is good and explain why. If something is a red flag, call it a red flag in plain English.

**No AI-flavoured filler.** Delete on sight: "It's worth noting that," "It is important to understand that," "In the context of," "This underscores the fact that," "delve into," "nuanced," "multifaceted," "comprehensive," "robust" (unless describing a specific technical property), "leveraging," "ecosystem" (unless referring to an actual biological system). These phrases signal that the writer did not know what to say.

**No hedging for its own sake.** "May," "might," "could potentially," "it appears that" — use these only when genuine epistemic uncertainty exists and you are flagging it explicitly. Do not soften a conclusion you are confident in because it sounds more measured.

**Directness.** State the verdict first. Explain it second. Never bury the lede in a paragraph of context-setting. If the thesis fails, say so in the first sentence of that section.

**Numbers over adjectives.** "Significant backlog growth" is useless. "Signed backlog grew by $145 million to $450 million YoY, with management confirming $120 million is scheduled for launch within the next 12 months" is useful. Always quantify where a number exists.

**Short sentences when making the most important points.** Long sentences for context. Short sentences for verdicts.

## STEP 0 — EXECUTION PROTOCOL (run this before writing a single word of analysis)

Do not begin scoring until you have completed all four steps below in order. Space-technology investing is plagued by pre-revenue hype and high-beta volatility; skipping this sequence is the most common reason analysis overrates unproven concepts or misses dilution traps.

_Pass 2 — Risk and anomaly signals._ Search separately for every occurrence of the following and extract verbatim with speaker name and quarter. A single hit on any of these terms requires explicit investigation before scoring proceeds:

**Step B — Fraud, short seller, and SPAC-stigma sweep**
Before scoring, run explicit web and X searches for:
- "[TICKER] short report" or "[TICKER] short seller"
- "[TICKER] fraud" or "[TICKER] accounting"
- "[TICKER] launch failure" or "[TICKER] satellite anomaly"

*Note: A high percentage of public space companies listed via SPACs (Special Purpose Acquisition Companies) and subsequently collapsed. Inspect the sponsor history and pre-merger projections closely.*

**Step C — The Capital Runway & Constellation Cost Audit**
Because space infrastructure is highly capital-intensive, you must compute the financial runway against deployment costs before scoring:
1. What is the current quarterly cash burn rate?
2. What is the unit cost to build and launch one satellite (or launch one rocket payload)?
3. What is the cost of the Minimum Viable Constellation (MVC) or launch manifest needed to reach cash-flow neutrality?
4. How many quarters of runway does the current cash balance provide?
5. Implied dilution: what percentage of current share capital must be issued to fund the MVC?

**Step D — Space Stack Layer Classification**
Classify [TICKER] into one or more of the following layers:
- **Layer L — Launch & Propulsion:** Rocket manufacturing, heavy/micro launch, orbital transfer vehicles (OTVs), thrusters, propellants.
- **Layer S — Spacecraft & Subsystems:** Satellite buses, solar arrays, reaction wheels, star trackers, thermal systems.
- **Layer C — Payload & Component:** Radiation-hardened processors, synthetic aperture radar (SAR), optical imagers, phased arrays, transponders, laser cross-links.
- **Layer O — Operations & Downlink:** Ground station networks, telemetry, command and control (TT&C) software, optical/RF downlink routing.
- **Layer D — Data & Connectivity:** Satellite-to-cell, LEO broadband, remote sensing, geospatial intelligence.
- **Layer G — Government & Cislunar:** Defence networks (SDA/NRO), NASA civil exploration, lunar landers, cislunar logistics.

## GATE CHECK — MARKET CAP & DILUTION FILTER

State the current market cap and enterprise value of [TICKER].
- Market cap: $___
- Enterprise value: $___

**Hard gate 1: If [TICKER] has a market cap above $5 billion, stop here. Do not continue.**
The space infrastructure sector is early-stage. Companies above $5B are already valued on fully realized constellations or launch monopolies (e.g. SpaceX equivalents). The asymmetric returns are found under this ceiling.

**Hard gate 2: Implied Dilution math check.**
Compute: (Capital needed for MVC – Current Cash) / Current Market Cap.
**If the implied dilution to reach cash-flow neutrality exceeds 35%, stop here. Do not continue.**
A business that must dilute current shareholders by more than 35% simply to deploy its minimum viable asset base is a capital trap, not an investment.

If it passes both gates, state:
- Realistic bull-case market cap in 24–36 months?
- Implied return multiple from today's price to that target? (Minimum acceptable: 5×)

## SECTION 0 — THE LAUNCH GATE & DE-RISKING TEST (Strait of Hormuz)

*Required — the thesis lives or dies here*

Map the access path to space for [TICKER]:
1. What is [TICKER]'s primary launch vehicle?
2. Are they dependent on a single provider (e.g. SpaceX) for launch?
3. What alternative launch options are qualified and available?
4. How long would a launch vehicle grounding (e.g. Falcon 9 FAA suspension) delay [TICKER]'s deployment schedule?
5. Launch manifest status: does [TICKER] have signed, deposit-backed launch contracts for its next 24 months of planned deployments?
6. Switching costs: how long would it take to re-qualify the payload on an alternative launcher (e.g. moving from Falcon 9 to Rocket Lab Neutron or Vulcan)?

**Required verdict:** SECURED LAUNCH PATH / CONSTRAINED LAUNCH PATH / LAUNCH ACCIDENT RISK

If LAUNCH ACCIDENT RISK (no manifest bookings, or payload is single-vehicle locked with zero backups), stop. The project is an operational gamble, not an infrastructure asset.

## SECTION 3 — BACKLOG QUALITY VS. MANIFEST REALISATION

*2 points — highest weighted category*

**Sub-section A — Trailing documented evidence**
1. Reported backlog for the last 4 quarters — provide the table.
2. What percentage of the backlog is backed by cash deposits or binding government task orders?
3. Any contract cancellations, manifest slips, or target date deferrals in the past 12 months?

**Sub-section B — Forward run-rate signals**
1. Has management stated in the last three earnings calls that they have secured launch slots and payload integration windows for the upcoming manifest? Quote verbatim.
2. What has management said about the conversion rate of backlog to near-term revenue?
3. What is the satellite build rate (units/month) and does it match the launch manifest pace?

**Scoring logic:**
Management confirming that 100% of next-stage payloads are manufactured and launched on-schedule with deposits paid = 2/2. A growing backlog with slipping launch dates or unbuilt satellites is a red flag.

**Score 2:** Backlog growing AND launch slots secured AND manufacturing cadence matches launch manifest. **Score 1:** Backlog growing but launch manifest is tight or subject to moderate slips. **Score 0:** Backlog declining, or manifest dates are speculative with no contracted launch slots.

## SECTION 12 — ADVERSARIAL TESTING: STEEL-MAN THE BEAR CASE

*Required — no score*

2. **The Obsolescence Threat:** Who is deploying a superior orbital architecture right now? How fast can they out-scale [TICKER]?
3. **The Customer Concentration Risk:** Model the loss of the primary government contract (e.g. NASA or SDA task order). Does the business survive?
4. **Funding freeze:** If capital markets close entirely to high-beta space names for 18 months, does [TICKER] go bankrupt?

**Rate the overall bear case:** WEAK / MODERATE / STRONG
