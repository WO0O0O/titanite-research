## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

**Supply chain map for glass interposer / TGV wafers:**

1. **Upstream (raw materials):** Borosilicate glass blanks, quartz, fused silica — sourced from Schott AG, AGC, Nippon Electric Glass, Corning
2. **PLANOPTIK's position:** Takes in raw glass wafers and performs precision structuring — grinding, lapping, polishing, through-hole etching, cavity formation, VLIS (Volume Laser Induced Structuring) in partnership with 4JET microtech, anti-reflection coatings. Output: structured glass wafers, TGV interposers, MEMS-grade glass substrates, microfluidic chips
3. **Downstream customers:** MEMS foundries, OSAT packaging houses, sensor manufacturers, semiconductor packaging integrators, microfluidics device makers. Named publicly: Infineon, Bosch, Samsung, Honeywell (as customer-base examples, not confirmed volume relationships)
4. **Hyperscaler end-use:** TGV glass interposers feed into advanced 2.5D/3D chip packaging, co-packaged optics (CPO), and RF/photonic integration — all of which are infrastructure layers for AI data centres running NVIDIA H100/B100/R100-class GPUs
5. **If PLANOPTIK disappeared tomorrow:** MEMS and advanced packaging customers would need to qualify a European or Asian alternative. Lead time for qualification is typically 6–18 months for precision glass components. Short-term production disruption at affected MEMS and sensor makers; medium-term impact on glass interposer supply chains if volume ramp had begun
6. **Competitive landscape:** Corning (US, $40bn market cap), SCHOTT AG (private, German), AGC (Japan), Tecnisco (Japan), Samtec (US, private) in broader glass substrates. For precision-structured glass wafers at European small-scale specialty volumes: PLANOPTIK is one of very few qualified European suppliers with in-house TGV capability
7. **Strait of Hormuz test:** PLANOPTIK does not control a dominant share of global glass wafer volume — Corning, AGC, and SCHOTT dwarf its output. However, in the niche of European-manufactured, precision-structured glass and quartz wafers for MEMS, sensors, and emerging TGV interposers below 300mm, PLANOPTIK is one of a very small number of qualified producers. Estimated share of this specific sub-segment: 5–15% globally, higher in Europe
8. **Switching costs:** Qualifying an alternative precision glass supplier takes 6–18 months, involves full process re-qualification, and requires the customer to re-certify the new substrate in their device. This is a meaningful moat for existing customers; it is not a wall for new competitors entering the broader market

**Required verdict: PARTIAL CHOKEPOINT**

PLANOPTIK is not a monopoly or duopoly controlling a critical global flow. It is a specialist sub-supplier with real but bounded moat characteristics. The thesis is not dead on this finding — partial chokepoints in emerging technology markets can still deliver strong returns if volume scale is reached before the broader market awakens — but the Strait of Hormuz test does not produce a slam-dunk verdict. ## SECTION 1 — WHICH AI INFRA BOTTLENECK DOES IT SOLVE?

**Score: 1/1 — Moderate evidence**

**Bottleneck addressed: Advanced packaging / substrates and optical interconnect**

As AI GPU clusters scale beyond 100kW per rack, organic ABF substrates are hitting physical limits: warpage at elevated temperatures, inadequate flatness for sub-micron lithography, insufficient dielectric performance for high-frequency signal routing. Glass substrates — with coefficient of thermal expansion (CTE) closer to silicon, superior flatness, and lower dielectric loss — are the industry-identified successor material.

Intel has publicly committed to glass core substrates as part of its advanced packaging roadmap, with commercialisation targeted for the 2029–2030 window. TSMC and Samsung are developing their own glass interposer lines. The qualification and process development work happening now in 2025–2026 is the upstream prerequisite for volume production from 2028 onwards.

PLANOPTIK supplies precision-structured glass and quartz wafers that serve as the substrate layer for TGV interposers — the foundational component in this transition. Additionally, its glass components feed into co-packaged optics (CPO) and photonic integration packages where glass provides the low-loss medium for optical signal routing between dies.

The bottleneck is real and quantifiable: the specialised glass semiconductor substrate market (distinct from commodity display glass) is valued at approximately $1.8 billion in 2025, growing at 13–67% CAGR through 2034 depending on the sub-segment, with the glass-core/TGV segment specifically at the higher end of that range. PLANOPTIK directly addresses this constraint, though as a niche supplier rather than a primary scale enabler at this stage.

**Score: 1/1**

1. **China supply chain exposure:** PLANOPTIK's production is entirely Europe-based (Germany and Hungary). Raw glass inputs come primarily from European and Japanese suppliers (SCHOTT, AGC, Nippon Electric Glass). No disclosed Chinese supply chain dependency.

## SYNTHESIS: THE ONE-PARAGRAPH PITCH

PLANOPTIK AG (P4O, ~€61m market cap) is a precision glass wafer specialist with in-house TGV (Through-Glass Via) structuring capability, operating as a partial chokepoint in the European advanced-packaging materials supply chain. Its VLIS (Volume Laser Induced Structuring) process — co-developed with 4JET microtech — is one of very few commercially available methods for producing crack-free, high-density TGV wafers in small-to-mid volumes, a capability that sits directly upstream of the glass-core substrate transition Intel has publicly targeted for 2029–2030 commercialisation. Management has guided for material revenue acceleration from 2027 as multiple undisclosed new customers enter series production, backed by a 5× Hungary capacity expansion and a KfW growth loan — concrete capital actions, not promotional language. The stock trades at approximately €13.45 with no sell-side coverage, having recently uplisted to the Regulated Market, meaning institutional discovery is structurally pre-Phase 1. The critical weakness at current price: the stock has already 4×'d from its €3.18 trough, and return mathematics from current levels max out at approximately 3–4× in a high bull case — below the framework's 500% threshold. The thesis is not broken; the entry point has narrowed. Watchlist with a buy trigger at confirmed series production announcements with named customers, or a retracement toward €8–10 on macro weakness that reopens the asymmetry.
