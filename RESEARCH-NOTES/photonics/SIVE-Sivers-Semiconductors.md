## SECTION 0 — THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ TEST

**Supply chain mapping: InP DFB laser arrays for co-packaged optics**

1. **Upstream:** Indium phosphide (InP) substrates — supplied primarily by Sumitomo Electric, JX Metals, and AXT/Tongmei. Sivers operates its own InP epitaxy capability in Glasgow, providing partial raw-material independence. Gallium and indium inputs subject to China export-control regime (controls on indium phosphide substrates introduced February 2025), but Sivers' Glasgow vertical integration provides meaningful insulation.

2. **Sivers' position:** Takes InP substrates in, performs molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) and metal-organic chemical vapour deposition (MOCVD) to grow InP laser structures, then fabricates DFB laser arrays using its proprietary InP100 process platform on 4-inch wafers. Produces finished compound semiconductor laser chips and arrays. For volume manufacturing, transfers designs to Win Semiconductor (Taiwan) as foundry partner.

3. **Downstream:** Silicon photonics transceiver modules (pluggable and co-packaged). These modules cannot function without an external III-V light source — silicon cannot emit light efficiently. Without SIVE's (or equivalent) DFB laser chips, the module is inoperable. Direct module integrators include Jabil (confirmed, April 2026), O-Net Technologies, Enablence Technologies, and POET Technologies. These feed into hyperscaler transceiver supply chains.

4. **End-use:** AI data centre GPU-to-GPU interconnects, specifically: 800G and 1.6T optical links within AI clusters. CPO architectures from Intel/Marvell/Ayar Labs ecosystems. The target end-buyers are AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, Meta, and Oracle — the five largest AI infrastructure spenders.

5. **If Sivers disappeared tomorrow:** Module integrators downstream would need to qualify an alternative InP DFB laser supplier. That qualification cycle at a hyperscaler tier takes 12–24 months minimum. In the near term, only Lumentum and Coherent have the established capacity to absorb demand — and both are already supply-constrained.

6. **Competitors:** Lumentum (dominant, ~50–60% EML market share), Coherent Corp (6-inch InP, massive scale), MACOM (Etched Facet Technology, GaAs-hybrid approach), Sumitomo Electric, Mitsubishi Electric, Applied Optoelectronics. This is an oligopoly, not a monopoly. Sivers is a specialist in DFB CW laser arrays for CPO/ELS specifically — a niche within the oligopoly where Lumentum is less dominant.

7. **Strait of Hormuz test:** Sivers does not currently pass a quantified percentage of global InP laser flow. It is a pre-volume player. The market for external light sources for CPO was approximately $1.8 billion in 2025. Sivers' 2025 revenue was SEK 306.6 million (~$29 million), meaning it accounts for approximately 1.6% of that market today. Qualified chokepoint status is contingent on volume ramp — not yet proven at scale.

8. **Switching costs:** 12–24 months for a hyperscaler-tier customer to qualify an alternative InP DFB supplier, given MBE process specificity, photonic integration testing, and thermal/power characterisation requirements.

**Verdict: PARTIAL CHOKEPOINT**

Sivers is not yet a full chokepoint by flow percentage — too small in revenue terms. However, its technology is a structural input that cannot be bypassed or substituted quickly, and it is accumulating design wins in CPO ecosystems where it will become a bottleneck as volume scales. The partial chokepoint verdict reflects current state; a full re-score post-volume-ramp initiation (expected 2027) is warranted.

## SECTION 1 — WHICH AI INFRA BOTTLENECK DOES IT SOLVE?

**Score: 1/1 | Evidence: Strong**

The bottleneck is optical interconnect — specifically, the external light source problem for co-packaged optics.

Silicon photonics integrates passive optical functions (routing, modulation) on silicon chips but cannot generate light. InP DFB laser arrays are the only commercially viable III-V solution for providing the continuous-wave light source. As AI data centres transition from 400G pluggable to 800G and 1.6T CPO architectures — driven by GPU-to-GPU bandwidth requirements that now exceed what electrical interconnects can provide — the demand for InP laser arrays scales directly with the number of optical ports deployed.

Each AI server rack in a 100kW+ deployment now requires hundreds to thousands of DFB laser emitters. NVIDIA's GB200 NVL72 rack interconnect fabric alone requires optical transceivers at a scale that is straining the entire InP laser supply chain. Lumentum's CEO publicly noted in Q4 2025 that demand for EML and CW laser chips was outpacing their production expansion capacity. Coherent received multi-hundred-million-dollar advance payments from NVIDIA to lock in capacity.

Sivers is not a peripheral beneficiary — InP DFB laser arrays are the primary enabling component. Without them, the transceiver module does not exist.

**Score: 1**

## SECTION 10 — SECULAR AND CYCLICAL TAILWINDS

**Score: 1/1 | Evidence: Strong**

The optical interconnect bottleneck is structural and irreversible. Silicon cannot generate light. Every AI data centre operating above approximately 100W per GPU requires optical interconnect, and every optical interconnect requires a III-V semiconductor laser. As AI compute clusters scale from 10,000-GPU to 100,000-GPU configurations — a trajectory that is infrastructure-constrained, not demand-constrained — total InP DFB laser unit demand scales linearly with port count. This demand survives a 30% AI capex cut. Even a severe AI investment slowdown does not reduce the optical content per GPU or reduce the number of ports that require a laser source. It may delay the ramp by 6–12 months; it does not eliminate the structural demand.

The current CPO/photonics upcycle is driven by the transition from 400G to 800G and 1.6T transceiver standards. The trough was 2023–2024 as the post-5G wireless correction hit SIVE's wireless revenues. The photonics cyclical recovery began in earnest in H2 2025 and is now the primary revenue driver. Both tailwinds are compounding simultaneously, as the framework requires.
