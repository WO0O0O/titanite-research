## The Core Rule
* **Buy / Build Positions:** Do this right at the market close (3:45 PM – 4:00 PM EST).
* **Sell / Exit Positions:** Do this right at the market open (9:30 AM EST) or just before the morning chaos kicks off.

## When to Use This (Do This IF...)
* **Standard, Quiet Trading Days:** Use this on normal days to capture the natural overnight price lift while avoiding daytime downward drift.
* **The Stock Had an Intraday Sell-Off:** Positive overnight reversals are historically strongest on nights following a heavy daytime sell-off. When market makers are caught holding inventory at the end of the day, prices regularly adjust upward overnight.
* **You are Using Market-on-Close (MOC) Orders:** Use this if your broker easily supports MOC orders. It guarantees you get filled right at the official closing auction print where institutional liquidity is deepest.
* **The Stock is in a Clean Uptrend:** Only time your entries this way if the target company is fundamentally solid and trading safely above its 200-day moving average.

## When to Avoid This (Don't Do This IF...)
* **Earnings Reports Are Dropping:** Skip it if there is an earnings release tonight or tomorrow morning. Massive gap risk around corporate earnings makes the overnight move completely unpredictable—it can easily gap down and trap you before the open.
* **Major Macro Data Releases Are Due:** Avoid doing this right before major macroeconomic announcements (like CPI inflation numbers or Fed rate decisions) that drop first thing in the morning.
* **The VIX is Exploding (High Market Panic):** If the broader market is in a panic, opening spreads will widen aggressively. The execution friction and slippage of trying to exit at 9:30 AM will destroy any theoretical overnight edge.
* **Low-Volume / Tiny Micro-Caps:** If a stock has thin volume, the bid-ask spread at open will be massive. Stick to assets with deep institutional volume blocks where you can slip in and out cleanly.

## The Source & Quick Explanation
* **The Paper:** *Nothing to See Here: How to Say It When You Need to* (Bruce Knuteson)
* **The TL;DR:** Decades of global stock data prove an incredibly persistent anomaly: almost 100% of the stock market's net gains over the last 30 years happen overnight (from close to the next day's open). If you buy at open and sell at close every day, your returns are historically flat or negative.
* **Why It Happens:** The opening bell is chaotic, driven by emotional retail orders and wider spreads. The closing cross is highly efficient, filled with massive institutional block orders. By entering at the close, you buy when liquidity is deepest and immediately position your capital to ride the overnight drift.
